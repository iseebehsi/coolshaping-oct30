What is CoolSculpting?

What is CoolSculpting?

CoolSculpting is a procedure that uses vacuum suction to target and shrink fat cells in your body. The suction pulls the fat out of the tissue gradually, which naturally and gradually breaks apart the fat cell. This destroys the fat cell over time, and your body then naturally absorbs and eliminates these dead fat cells.

Instead of a surgical operation, CoolSculpting is a non-invasive and non-surgical procedure that requires no incisions or recovery time. This is a great benefit, as CoolSculpting can be used to treat areas such as the abdomen, hips, back, thighs, and buttocks, which are common problem areas for many people.

This procedure can be used to treat fat in areas that are difficult to treat with diet and exercise. CoolSculpting is FDA-cleared, which means it has been extensively studied and has been proven effective.

This treatment is safe, effective, and non-invasive, so it's ideal for men and women of all ages who are looking for fast and effective results. CoolSculpting is a great option if you want to eliminate troublesome body fat without having to undergo surgery.

What are the benefits of CoolSculpting

CoolSculpting is a non-invasive fat reduction treatment that freezes away unwanted fat without surgery or downtime.

The treatment is ideal for people looking to smooth away areas of stubborn fat, especially those with isolated bulges or bulges that resist diet and exercise, as an alternative or complement to liposuction surgery.

During your treatment, you'll receive a gel pad applicator that's applied to the treatment area. Then, a cooling panel will chill the pad to a temperature that is cold, but not freezing. You'll feel a cold sensation, but nothing too uncomfortable.

The treatment is designed to target and destroy fat cells in your treated areas. Your fat cells are then gradually broken down and eliminated from your body over the course of several weeks following your treatment.

Throughout your treatment, we'll monitor your progress and offer guidance on optimal treatment techniques.

Following your treatment, you can immediately return to work and resume normal activities. However, you may still be a bit tender, and it may be a bit swollen at first. Over the next several weeks, you'll notice your treated area gradually smooth out as your body eliminates fat cells.

How does CoolSculpting work?

CoolSculpting is a non-invasive treatment that freezes fat cells in the body, causing them to die and fade from sight over the coming weeks.

The procedure involves applying cool paddles to the targeted area, which draws heat away from the fat cells and kills them. Over time, the affected cells will die and fade from sight, leaving you with a slimmer-looking physique.

During the treatment, the patient stays awake and typically reads or talks on their smartphone. Most treatments take between 35 and 60 minutes to complete, depending on the size of the area being treated.

CoolSculpting targets fat cells in the subdermal layer of skin. Although other non-invasive fat-reduction procedures also target fat cells, CoolSculpting is the only one that does so in the subdermal layer. Other procedures such as liposuction and laser fat reduction target fat cells above the subdermal layer, requiring the patient to undergo a more invasive procedure.

CoolSculpting is FDA-approved and is considered a safe, effective option for slimming down. Studies have shown that CoolSculpting treatment causes little or no harm to the skin or surrounding tissues.

Although CoolSculpting is FDA-approved, it's important to note that results can vary depending on each patient's body. Patients who undergo multiple treatments over an extended period of time tend to see the best results.

What are the side effects of CoolSculpting?

CoolSculpting is a procedure that uses cryolipolysis to reduce excess fat. While this procedure is known for being safe and effective, it does have some side effects you should be aware of.

These side effects generally last only a few days after the procedure and can be treated at home with over-the-counter medication.

These side effects include:

Temporary numbness: During the procedure, the technician may apply a numbing agent to your skin, such as lidocaine. This helps reduce any discomfort you may experience during the procedure.

Swelling: While the treatment itself doesn't cause much swelling, some patients may experience swelling in the treated area. This swelling should go away on its own within a few days.

Bruising: Your skin may be bruised after treatment, but this should go away on its own in a few days.

Numbness: The majority of patients experience temporary numbness in the treated area. This usually goes away within a few weeks.

Other side effects may include:

• Fatigue

• Mild discomfort

• Cold sensations



How long does the CoolSculpting treatment take?

The CoolSculpting treatment can take anywhere from 35 minutes to 60 minutes per area.

It's important to note that the number of sessions needed varies from person to person. Some people may see results after one treatment, while others may need several. Your doctor will be able to determine the ideal number of sessions for you.
